How To Write A Reusme:

Step 1:

To craft a standout resume, thorough preparation is crucial. Align your qualifications with the job requirements by carefully reviewing and organizing a comprehensive list that includes:

  • Noteworthy achievements from past roles showcasing your contributions.
  • Inventory of soft, hard, and technical skills aligned with job requirements.
  • Detailed past employer information: names, dates, locations, titles, responsibilities.
  • Educational credentials: degrees, certifications, licenses.
  • Relevant volunteer work experience.
  • Awards and honors demonstrating exceptional abilities.

Step 2:

Once your information is ready, select a resume format that highlights your qualifications effectively. Three standard formats are available, each organizing the same resume sections differently. Choose the one that suits your work experience level and professional goals best. Details of each format are provided below.

  • The chronological resume format is widely used, ideal for emphasizing work experience by placing it at the forefront. It suits candidates with steady professional backgrounds and no gaps in employment.
  • The functional resume format emphasizes skills and training to showcase your value to a team, suitable for those entering the workforce or changing careers. It is beneficial for individuals with employment gaps or limited work experience.
  • The combination resume format, or hybrid resume, blends functional and chronological formats. It lets you highlight skills and relevant work experience, ideal for experienced professionals and those aiming for career advancement.

Step 3:

Add your contact information

Ensure clear display of contact information on a resume for hiring managers to reach you. Include full name, phone number, professional email, city, state, ZIP code, and links to social media or portfolio. Explore resume examples for inspiration on formatting contact information. Sample resume header provides guidance on structuring contact details effectively.

Step 4:

Write a compelling resume summary

Here are some key components:

  • Resume Summary: Brief summary of qualifications, skills, and career goals.
  • Resume Objective: Used for first-time job seekers, career changers, or those returning to work after a break.
  • Modern Resume Writing: Emphasizes professional summary or profile section over traditional objective statement.
  • Objective Statement: If included, focus on company's needs rather than individual goals.

    Final step - Final Product

    Typos and grammatical errors are prevalent in resumes and are considered deal-breakers by approximately 80% of hiring managers. It is crucial to meticulously review your resume to ensure accuracy in content, including dates and company names. Additionally, formatting errors can deter hiring managers, so it is essential to follow basic formatting tips to make your resume easy to read.

  • Aim for a one-page resume unless you have over 10 years of experience, then two to three pages are acceptable.
  • Set margins to 1 inch; adjust to half an inch for additional content.
  • Use standard fonts like Times New Roman, Arial, or Helvetica.
  • Maintain font sizes between 10-12 points for the body and 14-16 points for headers.
  • Opt for single to 1.5-point line spacing.
  • Save your resume as a PDF or DOCX file, following employer preferences.
  • Name your resume file as "[Your First Name][Your Last Name][Resume].pdf" for clarity and organization.
